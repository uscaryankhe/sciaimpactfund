Strategy

Institutional Process.
Generational Conviction.

We apply the same analytical rigor as top-tier institutional funds — structured diligence, centralized decision-making, and a deployment philosophy built around decades, not quarters.

Mission

Capital. Legacy.
Compounded.

SCIA Impact Fund is the official social impact investment and philanthropy arm of Southern California Indo-Americans (USC SCIA). We deploy capital into high-potential Indian startups and mission-driven nonprofits in education, healthcare, economic empowerment, and South Asian community development.

Our model is built for generational legacy: the full portfolio and cash reserves are gifted directly to the next e-board, ensuring compounding impact for decades. Every check written is a chapter in a continuum that outlasts any single class.

Approach

Four Pillars of
Deployment.

01
Professional-Grade Process

Genuine venture and private equity framework: deal sourcing, structured diligence, term evaluation, and portfolio monitoring. The same rigor expected of top-tier institutional funds — applied with student conviction.

02
Dual Mandate Architecture

Equity investments into high-potential startups and strategic philanthropic grants into mission-aligned nonprofits treated as a unified deployment strategy. Not separate programs. A single, integrated mandate.

03
Centralized Decision-Making

Capital allocation grounded in rigorous diligence and informational edge. Centralized leadership. Measured conviction. No committee drift, no cultural sentiment, no deployment pressure from arbitrary timelines.

04
Asymmetric Compounding

We think in decades. The portfolio handed to the next class is the most important return metric we track. Short-cycle noise is ignored. Generational compounding is the mandate. Each e-board builds on what came before.

Philosophy

"We do not optimize for the current cycle. We optimize for the portfolio that the next generation inherits — compounding capital, institutional knowledge, and South Asian conviction across decades."
